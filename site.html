How to spot a scam website?

It's fairly easy to spot a scam website. Here are some of the most common red flags:

  • Extremely low prices. If the prices seem too good to be true, they probably are. Scammers lure people in by promising incredible deals.
  • Huge inventory in stock. If the website has a huge inventory with nothing out of stock, it's a red flag. Scammers lure in more people by having a larger inventory.
  • Obvious spelling/grammar problems, poor design. If the website looks like it was slapped together in a rush, it was probably was. Scammers create new websites all the time. Legitimate businesses have one website, and they invest time and attention in it.
  • New domain. If you do a WHOIS lookup on the domain and find it was registered very recently, that indicates the business may not be legitimate. Scammers must create new websites as their old ones get shut down.
  • Zero purchase protection. If the website says it only accepts payment by Venmo, Zelle, Cash App, or Bitcoin, that's a very strong signal of fraud. Legitimate businesses accept at least some payment methods with purchase protection.
  • Refuses to use escrow. If the seller refuses to use escrow even if you cover the fee, it's usually because the seller is a scammer. Escrow protects buyers from scams, and protects sellers from chargebacks, so it benefits both sides.

To learn more, see our article on how to spot an online firearm scam.
